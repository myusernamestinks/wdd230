Visual Hierarchy

Amazon

Amazon.com

Visual hierarchy is a design principle that involves organizing elements on a page to guide the viewer's attention. It helps prioritize images or content by creating a clear path and emphasizing certain desired elements over others. It's about making some things stand out more than others to lead the viewer's eyes where you want them to go.

Rule of Thirds

National Geographic

nationalgeographic.com

The Rule of Thirds is a guideline in visual design. IT is used by dividing a picture into nine equal parts using two horizontal and two vertical lines. This creates a grid with nine sections. The idea is to have key elements placed at intersetions to make the overall screen more visually appealing to those that visit the site.

White Space and Clean Design

Google

Google.com

Whitespace is the empty space between and around things on a webpage. Clean design means keeping things simple and clear, focusing on what's important and avoiding unnecessary complexity.
